What does it mean to be a tester?

As a tester, you’ll play a crucial role in improving open-source projects. Here’s what you can expect.

Support Open Source

Contribute to projects that prioritise transparency and user privacy.

Time Commitment

Each session typically lasts 20-40 minutes. You can choose the sessions that fit your schedule.

Tasks

Participate in usability tests, try out new features before they’re released, and share your feedback through surveys or interviews.

Your Impact

Your insights will directly influence the development of open-source projects, making them more user-friendly for everyone.

Incentives

In appreciation of your time, you may receive gift cards, early access to new features, or other rewards.

Frequently Asked Questions

When will I be contacted?

We will contact you when we have a project that is fitting to your profile.

Do I need to have any skills to participate?

Not specific ones. You don’t have to be an expert or a tech savvy to sign up. We are interested in your experience and your usage of the products.

How is my data protected?

Your data is private and only visible to the research team who will moderate the testing sessions. Anything that is said or written during the sessions is confidential.

How does the reward process work?

After the testing has been concluded, we will share an online gift card with your for your feedback and your time.

Do I need a device for the testing?

As we conduct most our testing sessions online, you need to provide your own laptop or computer for the testing sessions.

Do I need to download anything beforehand?

You will be updated before the user testing session if you need to download anything.
